Advanced persistent threats are characterized by determined, well-resourced adversaries that pursue objectives over extended periods, adapt to defensive pressure, and work to maintain enough access to achieve mission goals. That definition carries a practical implication for detection engineering: isolated alerts rarely capture the full sequence of actions, because the campaign is designed to look like routine administration and ordinary application behavior until enough small steps are assembled into coherent evidence.
