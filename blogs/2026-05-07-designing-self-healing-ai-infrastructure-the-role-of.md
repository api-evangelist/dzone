When Incident Response Becomes the Bottleneck Reliability engineering has historically relied on a predictable workflow. A monitoring system detects an anomaly, an alert is triggered, and an engineer investigates logs and metrics before applying a remediation step. This model works reasonably well for traditional applications where failures occur slowly and are relatively easy to diagnose. AI-driven systems behave differently. Modern AI platforms are built on layers of interconnected services.
